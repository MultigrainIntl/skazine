Rocksteady

The Bridge

Slower tempos, deeper bass, and soulful harmonies. Rocksteady lasted just two years but its influence on reggae and R&B runs through everything that followed.

Era
1966–1968
Origin
Kingston, Jamaica

The Story of Rocksteady

Rocksteady arrived in Jamaica around 1966 as ska's frenetic pace gave way to a more deliberate, bass-heavy groove. The horns pulled back. Vocal harmonies came forward. Producers and artists like Alton Ellis, The Heptones, and Phyllis Dillon defined an era with smooth, soulful deliveries rooted in American soul. Lyrically, rocksteady tackled social themes — poverty, inequality, rude boy culture — setting a precedent for reggae. It lasted barely two years but produced some of the most enduring recordings in Jamaican music history.
