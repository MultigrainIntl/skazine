Featured Artist Ska-Punk Denver, CO

Checkline Collective

Denver's rising ska-punk outfit blending classic Jamaican offbeats with modern punk energy and brass-heavy arrangements. The cornerstone of Colorado's growing ska scene since 2022.

See Upcoming Shows Book / Contact
Biography

Checkline Collective formed in Denver in the summer of 2022 when vocalist Marcus Webb and guitarist Dani Torres — veterans of separate punk bands — started trading ska records at a record fair in RiNo. Within six months they'd recruited a seven-piece lineup and were playing the Larimer Lounge.

Their sound draws directly from the classic Jamaican ska template — choppy guitar upstrokes, walking bass, syncopated brass — but accelerated to punk tempos and filtered through the social urgency of third-wave bands like Operation Ivy and Rancid. The horn section, anchored by trombone player James Okafor and trumpet player Sofia Chen, has become their signature: tight, driving, and never decorative.

The band self-released their debut EP, Mile High Skankin', in early 2024 to strong response within the Colorado scene. A second record is currently in progress.
